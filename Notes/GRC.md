  

## GRC Frameworks Overview

Organizations don't build their GRC programs from scratch — they leverage established **frameworks and standards** that provide structured approaches to governance, risk, and compliance.

#### What is a Framework?

A framework is a structured set of guidelines and best practices that organizations can adopt and tailor to their specific needs. Frameworks provide a common language and methodology.

#### Major GRC Frameworks

**ISO 27001 — Information Security Management System (ISMS)**

International standard for managing information security

Requires a systematic approach to managing sensitive information

Certification available through accredited bodies

Widely recognized globally

Annex A contains 93 controls across 4 themes

**NIST Cybersecurity Framework (CSF)**

Developed by the U.S. National Institute of Standards and Technology

Five core functions: Identify, Protect, Detect, Respond, Recover

Voluntary framework, widely adopted across industries

NIST CSF 2.0 added "Govern" as a sixth function

Free to use, no certification required

**SOC 2 (Service Organization Control)**

Developed by AICPA (American Institute of CPAs)

Five Trust Service Criteria: Security, Availability, Processing Integrity, Confidentiality, Privacy

Type I: Point-in-time assessment

Type II: Assessment over a period (usually 6-12 months)

Critical for SaaS and cloud service providers

**COBIT (Control Objectives for Information Technology)**

IT governance framework by ISACA

Helps align IT with business goals

Covers 40 governance and management objectives

Useful for IT audit and compliance

**PCI DSS (Payment Card Industry Data Security Standard)**

Required for organizations handling credit card data

12 requirements across 6 categories

Annual assessments required

Non-compliance can result in heavy fines

**HIPAA (Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act)**

U.S. regulation for protecting healthcare data

Privacy Rule and Security Rule

Applies to covered entities and business associates

Penalties up to $1.5M per violation category per year

```
Framework Selection Guide:
─────────────────────────────────────────
Need                    → Framework
─────────────────────────────────────────
Global InfoSec cert     → ISO 27001
U.S. cybersecurity      → NIST CSF
SaaS/Cloud vendor       → SOC 2
Credit card handling    → PCI DSS
Healthcare data         → HIPAA
IT governance           → COBIT
─────────────────────────────────────────
```

#### Choosing the Right Framework

Most organizations adopt multiple frameworks based on:

Industry requirements

Customer expectations

Regulatory obligations

Geographic presence

Organizational maturity

## GRC Tools

Modern GRC programs rely on specialized tools and platforms to automate workflows, track compliance, and manage risk at scale.

#### Why GRC Tools Matter

Manual GRC management using spreadsheets quickly becomes unsustainable as organizations grow. GRC tools provide:

Centralized risk and compliance management

Automated evidence collection

Real-time dashboards and reporting

Audit trail and documentation

Workflow automation for policy reviews and approvals

#### Common Features in GRC Tools

```
Feature                  Description
─────────────────────────────────────────────
Risk Register            Track and manage identified risks
Control Library          Maintain a catalog of security controls
Evidence Collection      Automated gathering of compliance proof
Policy Management        Create, review, approve, and distribute policies
Audit Workflows          Plan, execute, and track audit activities
Dashboards               Real-time visibility into risk & compliance posture
Vendor Management        Assess and monitor third-party risks
Incident Management      Track and respond to security incidents
```

#### Getting Started Without Enterprise Tools

For smaller organizations or learning purposes, you can start with:

**Spreadsheets** — Risk registers, control matrices, compliance trackers

**Notion/Confluence** — Policy documentation and procedures

**Jira/Asana** — Tracking remediation tasks

**Google Forms** — Self-assessment questionnaires
