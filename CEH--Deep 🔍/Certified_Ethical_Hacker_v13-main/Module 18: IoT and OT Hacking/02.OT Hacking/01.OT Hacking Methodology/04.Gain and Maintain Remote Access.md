# 🌐 Gain and Maintain Remote Access 
Attackers gain remote access to industrial control systems by exploiting vulnerabilities in protocols or injecting malware. Once inside, they can manipulate operations, cause physical and financial damage, and use the compromised devices to target other networked systems. To maintain access, attackers may clear logs, modify firmware, or install rootkits, enabling ongoing control and the ability to monitor or alter operations on devices like PLCs.

---
## Gaining Remote Access using DNP3 
Attackers can gain remote access to industrial control systems (ICS) by exploiting poorly secured networks or default/weak passwords. Using tools like Shodan, they scan for open ports, such as DNP3 on port 20000, and identify vulnerabilities. Once an open port is found, attackers access the login interface and can use default credentials or brute-force passwords to take control of the ICS network or devices.
